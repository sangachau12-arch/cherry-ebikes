Press Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  ·  Media Contact: communications@yorku.ca

Toronto, ON — April 10, 2025

York University Launches New Student Innovation Hub at Keele Campus

The 12,000 sq ft facility will provide students with dedicated space for entrepreneurship, design, and cross-disciplinary collaboration — opening September 2025.

York University today announced the opening of the York Innovation Hub, a dedicated facility for student-led entrepreneurship and creative project development, set to open at the Keele Campus in September 2025. The hub is the result of a $4.2 million investment from the university and private partners.

The space will house state-of-the-art prototyping labs, recording studios, and co-working areas available to all enrolled students free of charge. Programming will include mentorship sessions with industry leaders, funding pitch competitions, and a rotating residency for student startups.

"This hub is a direct response to what our students have been telling us for years — they need real space to build real things. York is committed to making that happen."

— Dr. Rhonda Lenton, President & Vice-Chancellor, York University

Applications to reserve dedicated workspace in the hub open May 1, 2025 via the York student portal. The facility will officially launch with an open house on September 8, 2025.

About York University: York University is one of Canada's largest universities, offering an exceptional academic experience across more than 200 undergraduate and graduate programs. Located in Toronto, York is committed to research excellence, student success, and creating positive change in the world. Visit yorku.ca for more information.

Who We Are

Art that belongs
to everyone.

Toronto Arts Collective was founded on one belief: that art shouldn't be reserved for the few. Since 2016, we've been creating space — physical and otherwise — for emerging and established artists across every medium to create, connect, and be seen.

We run a gallery in Kensington Market, a year-round residency programme, community workshops, and a speaker series that brings the Toronto arts world into conversation with itself. Everything we do is rooted in accessibility. Most of our programming is free. All of it is for you.

Whether you're an artist looking for your next home, an art lover looking for your next favourite show, or someone just beginning to wonder if this world might be for you — you're in the right place.
